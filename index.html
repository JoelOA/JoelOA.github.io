Recognition of Walking Terrain Using Machine Learning

Joel Osei-Asamoah, Nathan Amanquah

IEEE 9th International Conference on Adaptive Science and Technology, 2024

Paper

TL;DR: A machine learning system using accelerometer and altimeter data to classify walking terrains, with an LSTM model achieving 96% accuracy by capturing temporal dependencies in time-series data, demonstrating potential for real-time deployment in low-cost wearable devices.

Limo Cobot OS Upgrade

Joel Osei-Asamoah, Kojo Anyinam-Boateng

Ongoing Research | Ashesi University | Aug 2025 - present

To improve the quality of the Introduction to AI and Robotics (CS353) course. I upgraded the Limo cobot platform from ROS2 Foxy to ROS2 Humble to give students experience with newer ROS distros. This involved disassembling the Limo to access the NVIDIA board's SSD, which I flashed with JetPack 6.1 to establish Ubuntu 22.04 as the new operating system. After installing ROS2 Humble, I systematically resolved package dependencies and API compatibility issues. I then validated the upgrade by testing all student lab exercises on the platform to ensure full functionality for educational use.

Drone Positioning System

Joel Osei-Asamoah, Kojo Anyinam-Boateng

Ongoing Research | Ashesi University | Aug 2025 - Present

Developing a modular, low-cost motion capture system for the CoDrone Pro using classical computer vision algorithms to detect IR markers and enable precise positioning. The system tracks the drone's location in 3D space and sends velocity commands for autonomous navigation to specific coordinates. This platform will serve as an educational tool for the Introduction to AI and Robotics (CS353) course, where students will program drones to autonomously navigate through predefined paths.

RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition

Competition | Ashesi University (ARC Lab) | Sept 2024 - May 2025

Developed perception and localization systems for F1TENTH Autonomous Racing Competition with the ARC lab. Created a sensor fusion state estimator (IMU + wheel encoders) and designed a LiDAR-based centerline extraction algorithm.
